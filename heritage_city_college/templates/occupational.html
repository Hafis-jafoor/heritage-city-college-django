Bachelor of Occupational Therapy

The occupational therapy department's flagship is the on-campus clinic, recognized for its outstanding facilitation. With state-of-the-art equipment, this facility offers students vital hands-on clinical experience essential for their future roles as occupational therapists. This experience not only improves their skills but also enriches their comprehension of the features and applications of each piece of equipment, ultimately contributing to the well-being of the patients they will serve in the future.

Opportunities

Obtaining an occupational therapy degree will prepare students for various career paths, including roles such as,

  • Occupational Therapy Aide
  • Speech Therapist
  • Specializing in Neuro-rehabilitation
  • Physiotherapy Assistant
  • Pediatric Therapist

Graduates of this program can find employment in diverse settings such as multi-specialty hospitals, rehabilitation centers, neuro-rehabilitation centers, geriatric homes or old age homes, private practices, private clinics, and various other healthcare environments.
